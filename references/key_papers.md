# Key References

## Core PBPK Framework

1. **Shah & Betts 2012** - Towards a platform PBPK model to characterize the plasma and tissue disposition of monoclonal antibodies in preclinical species and human. J Pharmacokinet Pharmacodyn 39:67-86.
   - Minimal PBPK model used as starting point
   - Physiological parameters, reflection coefficients, FcRn recycling fraction

2. **Ng et al. 2006** - Pharmacokinetics/pharmacodynamics of nondepleting anti-CD4 monoclonal antibody (TRX1) in healthy human volunteers. Pharm Res 23:95-103.
   - 2-pore PBPK framework for large molecules

3. **Jones et al. 2019** - A physiologically-based pharmacokinetic model for the prediction of monoclonal antibody pharmacokinetics from in vitro data. CPT:PSP 8:738-747.
   - Updated full tissue PBPK for mAbs

## FcRn Biology

4. **Pyzik et al. 2019** - The Neonatal Fc Receptor (FcRn): A Misnomer? Front Immunol 10:1540.
   - Comprehensive FcRn biology review

5. **Garg & Balthasar 2007** - Physiologically-based pharmacokinetic (PBPK) model to predict IgG tissue kinetics in wild-type and FcRn-knockout mice. J Pharmacokinet Pharmacodyn 34:687-709.
   - FcRn concentration estimates, recycling parameters

6. **Vaccaro et al. 2005** - Engineering the Fc region of immunoglobulin G to modulate in vivo antibody levels. Nat Biotechnol 23:1283-1288.
   - ABDEG technology foundation, pH-dependent FcRn binding

7. **Datta-Mannan 2019** - Mechanisms Influencing the Pharmacokinetics and Disposition of Monoclonal Antibodies and Peptides. Drug Metab Dispos 47:1100-1110.
   - FcRn recycling quantitative parameters

## Efgartigimod

8. **Ulrichts et al. 2018** - Neonatal Fc receptor antagonist efgartigimod safely and sustainably reduces IgGs in humans. J Clin Invest 128:4372-4386.
   - Phase 1 clinical data: PK + IgG reduction
   - 10 mg/kg IV: ~75% IgG reduction

9. **Howard et al. 2021** - Safety, efficacy, and tolerability of efgartigimod in patients with generalised myasthenia gravis (ADAPT): a multicentre, randomised, placebo-controlled, phase 3 trial. Lancet Neurol 20:526-536.
   - Phase 3 efficacy in gMG

## TMDD

10. **Mager & Jusko 2001** - General pharmacokinetic model for drugs exhibiting target-mediated drug disposition. J Pharmacokinet Pharmacodyn 28:507-532.
    - TMDD model framework

11. **Gibiansky et al. 2008** - Approximations of the target-mediated drug disposition model and identifiability of model parameters. J Pharmacokinet Pharmacodyn 35:573-591.
    - QSS approximation for TMDD

## Physiological Parameters

12. **Brown et al. 1997** - Physiological parameter values for physiologically based pharmacokinetic models. Toxicol Ind Health 13:407-484.
    - Comprehensive tissue volumes and blood flows

13. **ICRP Publication 89 (2002)** - Basic anatomical and physiological data for use in radiological protection: reference values.
    - Standard reference for tissue volumes

## Special Populations

14. **Malik & Edginton 2018** - Physiologically-based pharmacokinetic modeling of monoclonal antibodies in pediatric populations. CPT:PSP 7:e1.
    - Pediatric mAb PBPK scaling

## Sensitivity Analysis

15. **Saltelli et al. 2008** - Global Sensitivity Analysis: The Primer. Wiley.
    - SA methodology

16. **Zhang et al. 2015** - Sensitivity analysis and parameter identifiability of PBPK models. CPT:PSP 4:e27.
    - SA specifically for PBPK
